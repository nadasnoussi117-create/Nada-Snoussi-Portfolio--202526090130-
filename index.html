education & learning experience

Moving from Fes, Morocco to China at 19 years old to pursue a degree in Computer Science at Weifang University of Science and Technology has been the most challenging and rewarding experience of my life. As a first-year international student adjusting to a completely new culture and academic environment, this final semester has truly represented the culmination of my foundational growth. My educational journey in programming has been a transformative experience, shaping my logical thinking and problem-solving skills. Over the past academic year, I have immersed myself in various programming paradigms, focusing heavily on foundational concepts and practical applications. The core of my learning experience has been divided between understanding low-level memory management and logic through C programming, and exploring the dynamic, visual aspects of front-end web development.

Starting with C programming in my first semester, I was introduced to the fundamental building blocks of computer software. Learning C was initially challenging because of its strict syntax and manual memory management, especially while adapting to university-level courses. However, grasping concepts such as pointers, arrays, structures, and dynamic memory allocation provided me with a profound understanding of how computers execute instructions at a hardware level. I spent countless hours debugging code, resolving segmentation faults, and optimizing algorithms. Through platforms like HackerRank and SoloLearn, I continuously tested my knowledge against a variety of coding challenges. These platforms offered an interactive way to practice syntax, data structures, and algorithmic efficiency. Completing these challenges not only solidified my understanding of loops, conditionals, and functions but also taught me the importance of writing clean and efficient code. The satisfaction of finally passing all test cases for a complex problem in C was a major driving force in my educational journey as a computer science freshman.

Transitioning to web programming in this final semester opened a completely different dimension of software development. While C programming was rigid and logic-heavy, web development allowed for immediate visual feedback and creative freedom. I learned how to structure web pages using HTML, ensuring semantic accuracy and accessibility. I then moved on to CSS, discovering how to style elements, create responsive layouts, and apply visual aesthetics to make applications user-friendly. The shift from a console-based output to a fully interactive graphical interface was exciting. My coursework required me to build various web pages from scratch, combining structure and style to create cohesive digital experiences that feel modern and intuitive.

Beyond just writing code, my learning experience at Weifang University of Science and Technology has taught me resilience and the importance of continuous self-directed learning. Technology evolves rapidly, and the ability to independently research errors, read documentation, and understand new rules is just as crucial as knowing the syntax. Collaborating and working on individual projects taught me the value of code readability, file structuring, and creative problem-solving. I have realized that programming is not just about typing commands into an editor; it is about breaking down large, complex problems into small, manageable pieces.

Every assignment and exam served as a stepping stone, pushing me out of my comfort zone. Whether it was designing a responsive layout or writing algorithms in C, each task demanded patience and analytical thinking. As I wrap up this final semester of my first year, I am incredibly grateful for the structured coursework and the hands-on practice, as they have transformed me from a beginner into a capable, confident student. In conclusion, my education in both C programming and web development has equipped me with a versatile skill set. I can appreciate the deep, structural logic required for systems and the design-oriented thinking needed for web interfaces. This journey has established a strong foundation that I will carry forward into all my future software engineering endeavors.
